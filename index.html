Why

Institutions should evolve around the needs of human beings instead of the other way around. Blockchain technology offers the pieces of the puzzle to build these human centric institutions. Copernicus is an art project that explores the new paradigm of decentralization in the digital realm. By experimenting with shared ownership and (de)centralized governance the collective steering of money and power is explored.

What

Copernicus is a closed artwork made of glass that contains a private key that accumulates value. By buying a Copernicus token, one becomes 1/21 owner of the artwork and while doing so, one adds money to its treasury. Breaking the artwork gives access to the funds in the treasury. This raises interesting questions: What is shared ownership? What do we value (together)? And eventually, who decides about the faith of things that we own and govern collectively? Be invited to make this search with us.

How

One can become owner of one of 21 Copernicus-tickets, via the marketplace Open Sea (link). These tokens give unlimited access to places where the artwork is being displayed. Nine tokens are initially distributed over people that contributed to its realization, the other 12 are sold on Open Sea. At a price of 0,5 ether, the initial value of the artwork is (koers * 6 ether) euro. By being free to trade the tokens, the eventual value of the artwork will be decided by by the market.

Where

The artwork will be displayed at prominent places that underwrite the goals of Is it Copernicus and where the initiators are invited. This can be @ a museum but also @ conferences where the impact of tokenisation on the academic fields of law, economy or governance is discussed.
