Why

Our institutions should evolve around the needs of humans instead of the other way around. Blockchain technology offers the pieces of the puzzle to build these institutions.
Project Copernicus explores this new paradigm of decentralization of money and power by experimenting with shared ownership and (de)centralized governance with digital assets.

What

Copernicus is a closed glass artwork that contains a private key that accumulates value. By buying a token, people become 1/21 owner of the artwork and while doing so, they add money to its treasury. Breaking the artwork gives access to the funds. Yet, most and for all it raises other questions: What is ownership? What do we value (together)? And eventually, who decides about the faith of things that we own and govern collectively? Be invited to make this search with us.

How

People can become owner of one of 21 Copernicus-tickets. These tickets give unlimited access to places where the artwork is being displayed. 9 tokens are initially distributed over people that contributed to its realization, the other 12 are sold on the open market. At a price of 60 euro, the initial value of the artwork is 720 euro. By being free to trade the tokens, the eventual value of the artwork will be dictated by the market.

Where

The artwork will be displayed at prominent places that underwrite the goals of Project Copernicus. For now this is done/decided by its initiators, in the future all tokenholders will be able to vote and thus collectively be able to decide on the faith of the Artwork.
